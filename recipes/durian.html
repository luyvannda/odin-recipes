What is Durian?

Durian fruit is the edible fruit of several tree species belonging to the genus "Durio". It is distinctive for its large size, strong odour, and thorn-covered rind. It is commonly associated with Southeast Asian cuisine, especially in Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, Cambodia, Philippines, Bangladesh and Vietnam. Durian is native to Borneo and Sumatra, but it is also cultivated in Cambodia. There are three varieties of durian that are traditional in Cambodia: the "Ov Khak", "Sadong Kit" and "Dounta". These are similar to some Indonesian varieties of durian. Durian is one of the most popular fruits in Cambodia...

Durian Crepe Recipe

Nothing beats the taste of durian pillow crepes, a delicious dessert made of paper-thin pancakes stuffed with whipped cream and durian chunks. You can easily whip up a batch of these heavenly treats at home and enjoy them chilled whenever you crave for something light and creamy.

Ingredients

  • 150g all-purpose flour
  • 330ml fresh milk
  • 1/2 tablespoon butter, melted
  • 2 eggs
  • 400g of durian pulp
  • 250ml of whipping cream
